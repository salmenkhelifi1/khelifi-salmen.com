## Context

Building products and businesses is a continuous learning process. This article shares real experiences — not theoretical advice — from my journey building software products and running a freelance business.

Every lesson here comes from a mistake I made or an insight I had while building in public.

## The approach

### What I did

I approached this with a builder's mindset: ship fast, measure everything, and iterate based on real feedback rather than assumptions.

### What I learned

The process taught me several things that changed how I build:

- **Start with the problem** — Features don't matter if you're solving the wrong problem
- **Talk to users early** — The first version should be in front of real users within weeks, not months
- **Charge from day one** — Free users give different feedback than paying customers
- **Focus on distribution** — A great product with no distribution is just a hobby

## Results and takeaways

Looking back, here are the takeaways I keep coming back to:

- Speed is a competitive advantage — move fast even if it means imperfect code
- Revenue is the only validation that matters for a business
- Building in public compounds — every tweet and post is an asset
- The best decisions come from data, not opinion
